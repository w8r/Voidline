This project pursues not one, but many idealistic tasks:
- a taxonomy of everything
- machine-understandable representation of all things
- interactive pan-and-zoom map of all the data
- true natural language processing

It is only by these means, that higher, even more idealistic goals can be achieved, such as increased public knowledge and awareness, or artificial intelligence.

If the project reaches its potential, it would tremendously improve research systems, collaborative research, access to high-level and high-quality, up-to-date academic or scientific information, and generally the true "opening up" of knowledge and research to the general public.

It would allow anyone to easily see the big picture of things, how everything relates to each other, or how a particular piece fits into the system (whatever the context).

It would also allow for true search for information, using natural language, by retriving information from computers at a quality level that would be indistinguishable from that emitted by humans, even better.

Ultimately, it would connect all of us in the quest for knowledge, where everyone can work together to improve our collective understanding and knowledge of things.
